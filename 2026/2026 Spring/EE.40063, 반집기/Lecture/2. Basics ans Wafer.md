# Quiz

>[!a) The CMOS process we described in class could start with either a P-type or an N-type Si substrate Usually P-type is used. Why?]-
>
>P-type silicon substrates are commonly used because NMOS transistors have higher electron mobility than PMOS hole mobility. As a result, NMOS devices provide higher drive current and better preformance.
>
>By starting with a P-type substrate, NMOS transistors can be directly formed in the substrate, while PMOS transistors are fabricated in N-wells. This configuration simplifies the process integration and optimizes device performance since the higher-performance NMOS devices are formed directly in the bulk slicon.

>[!b) Why do modern CMOS processes typically start with a quite lightly doped wafer (P type , 5 X 10^(-20)  Ohm cm), as opposed to choosing a higher doping that would provide the correct threshold voltage for one of the MOS transistor types]-
>
>Modern CMOS processes start with lightly doped wafers to maintain better control of device characteristics and reduce unwanted effects.
>
>First, lightly doped substrates help reduce junction capacitance, which improves switching speed.
>
>Second, thershold voltage can be precisely adjusted later using channel implantation, allowing independent optimization of NMOS and PMOS devices. If the substrate were heavily doped initially, it would limit flexibility and degrade device performance due to higher electric fields and increased leakage.

>[!c) Explain the two main reasons why single crystal silicon is chosen as the wafer substrate material as opposed to polycrystalline or amorphous silicon?]-
>
>First, single-crystal silicon has very low defect density and no grain boundaries, which allows charge carriers to move with higher mobility and results in better electrical performance.
>
>Second, single-crystal silicon provides uniform and well-controlled electrical properties, which are essential for precise device fabrication and reliable large-scale integrated circuits. Polycrystalline or amorphous silicon contains grain boundaries and structural disorder that degrade carrier transport and device reliability
