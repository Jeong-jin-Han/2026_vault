>[!6.6 In the Deal Grove oxidation model, the flux F! is normally neglected. Explain what this flux is and why it is OK to ignore it in developing the linear parabolic model]-
>
>In the Deal-Grove oxidation model, F1 represents the transport of oxidant species ($O_2$ or $H_2O$) from the bulk gas phase to the oxide surface.
>
>This flux corresponds to mass transport through the gas boundary layer above the wafer.
>
>In most thermal oxidation furnaces, the oxidant gas is supplied with sufficient flow and mixing. Therefore, the concentration of oxidant at the gas-oxide interface quickly reaches equilibrium with the bulk gas concentration. As a result, the gas-phase transport resistance is very small compared with the other two processes: F2 (diffusion through the oxide layer), F3(chemical reaction at the Si/SiO2 interface)
>
>Since the oxidation rate is mainly controlled by oxide diffusion or interface reaction, the gas transport step is not rate-limiting. Therefore F1 can be neglected when derviing the linear-parabolic Deal-Grove oxidation model.


>[!6.7 In the Deal Grove oxidation model, explain why the oxidant flux through the SiO2 (F2) is drawn as a straight line]-
>
>The oxidant flux through the oxide layer (F2) follows Fick's First Law of diffusion
>
>$F_2 = -D \frac {dC}{dx}$
>In the Deal-Grove model, the following assumptions are made:
>- steady-state diffusion through the oxide
>- constant diffusion coefficient $D$
>- constant boundary concentrations at the oxide surface and the Si/SiO2 interface
>  
>  Under steady-state conditions, the flux is constant throughout the oxide layer. Therefore the concentration gradient $\frac{dC}{dx}$ must also be constant
>  
>  A constant concentration gradient results in a linear concentration profile, which is why the oxidant concentration through the oxide is drawn as a straight line from $C_s$ at the oxide surface to $C_i$ at the Si/SiO2 interface.
>- 
  

>[!6.8 In an experiment, an oxidation furnace is set up as shown below. The two wafers on the left are lying flat, while those on the right are standing vertically and are closely spaced. Using the Deal Grove oxidation model, explain what you think would result in this experiment in therms of the thickness of the oxides grown on the wafers]-
>
>In the furnace setup, the wafers on the left are lying flat, while the wafers on the right are standing vertically and closely spaced.
>
>In thermal oxidation, the oxidant must first be transported from the gas flow to the wafer surface before diffusing through the oxide layer. When wafers are placed vertically and closely space, the gas flow between wafers becomes restricted. This reduces the supply of oxidant to the wafer surfaces and increases the gas-phase mass transport resistance.
>
>As a result, the effective oxidant concentration at the oxide surface becomes lower for the closely spaced vertical wafers. According to the Deal-Grove model, a lower surface concentration reduces the oxidation flux and slows oxide growth.
>
>Therefore:
>- Flat wafers (left) -> better gas exposure -> 
>  higher oxidant concentration -> thicker oxide
>  
>- Closely spaced vertical wafers (right) -> limited oxidant supply -> thinner oxide
>   
>Thus, wafers with better oxidant access will grow thicker oxides.
